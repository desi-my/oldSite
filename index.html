Movie Poster

The goal of the project is to create a Poster for the movie Ice Age 2. The project is built entirely in Photoshop. I applied filters and other effects utilizing alpha channels, masks and paths to isolate selections & manipulate them. I built effects on effects for creative results. I used layer and channel masks to feather the edges of my images and to give the appearance of layers merging with each other, and to soften the transition. Opacity was using on some layers to control visibility of one layer to another. For flexibility and editing, I used blend modes and adjustment layers. I created my own water and ice texture as well as I created the flowers.

  • Duration: 1 week
  • Category: Print/Digital Design

Photo Retouch & Repair

The goal of the project is to retouch and repair an old damaged photo. I removed the color cast by using Levels to balance the colors. I assembled the pieces then created a “compilation layer” to make repairs. I used multiply mode temporarily for helping me to line up the pieces and showing where they best meet. I used the “Sample all layers” feature on some of the layers. I stole parts of the image and duplicate to repair missing areas. I utilized tools such as: Clone Stamp, Healing & Spot healing Brush, Patch Tool, Content-Aware Move tool.

  • Duration: 2 days
  • Category: Print/Digital Design

Sketch Sears Shop-Easy App

The Sears Shop-Easy application was created during the 2015 Sears Hackathon. The application was designed by a team of three people and was targeting an in-store customer shopping experience. The purpose of the application is to provide better customer support and improve the quality of the shopping process.

  • The Sears Shop-Easy application allows customers to do an express check out on their mobile device by scanning barcode of each item with their phone as they shop, instead of waiting in line at the register.
  • The Shop-Easy application allows customers to do an express check out on their mobile device by scanning barcode of each item with their phone as they shop, instead of waiting in line at the register.
  • This application allows the customer to keep track of the number of items in their cart, the price of each item, as well as a total price of the purchase.
  • At the mobile express checkout all bonus points and coupons can be applied with just a few clicks.
  • By scanning an item’s barcode with the application the customer can pull up a full description of the item and its rating.
  • In-store customer attendant can be requested with few easy steps through this application.
  • Duration: 2 days
  • Category: UX/UI Design
  • Team: Desislava Yovcheva, Jiyoon Seo, Daria Sidorko

Eval is a web tool, intended to be used by psychologists to allow them to more easily, and consistently do patient evaluations. The tool will contain a questionnaire that a psychologist can use to evaluate their patients, and will output a report that will contain an evaluation that the psychologist can use as it is, or adjust as needed to create a final evaluation. Psychologist will pay a subscription fee to have access to use the tool and generate its output. In addition, the questionnaire is not static. The tool will allow the owners/administrators of the web application to create/edit/ and adjust the questions, answers and outputs that the tool creates. There are three User Personas:

  • Psychologist - User who subscribes to the service and uses the surveys to evaluate patients. This the primary user of the EasyEval service functionality.
  • Author - User who has admin privileges to the Eval Web application to create and edit the surveys that are offered.
  • Admin - User who has admin privileges and attends to the non-survey functionality of the site. Like user maintenance and trouble shooting. The Author and the Admin will likely be the same person and have the same permissions on the first versions of Eval
  • Duration: 1 month
  • Category: UX/UI Design
